ColumnNameTypeFormula / SourceNotes
DTotal qty (payable)InputGross qty × payable% — from contractFor metals: Gross × (1−moisture%) × assay% × payable%. Store on contract.
EPriced qtyInputUpdated when QP lots are fixedStarts at 0. Increases as seller calls price during QP window. Max = D.
FUnpriced qtyFormula= D − ERemaining floating exposure. Drive colour coding from this field.
GFutures hedge qtyInputFrom trade blotter / hedge cardMatched against priced qty (priced rows) or unpriced qty (unpriced rows).
HHedge ratio %Formula= G/E (priced) or G/F (unpriced)100% = fully hedged. Flag if <80% on priced rows.
ITrade price (fixed)InputFrom contract when QP fixedNULL until price is fixed. MTM (col K) must be suppressed when NULL.
JMarket price (live)Lookup= Price Quote Master · Settlement columnOne lookup per commodity. Same price for physical and futures MTM on same row.
KPhysical MTM ($)Formula= (J−I) × dir_sign × E [Buy=+1, Sell=−1]ONLY when State=Priced or Partial AND trade price not NULL. Show text label otherwise.
LPhysical P&L ($)Formula= K (Phase 1)Phase 2: split Realised (settled/invoiced) + Unrealised (open).
MFutures entry priceInputFrom futures trade blotterPrice at which hedge futures contract was executed.
NFutures MTM ($)FormulaShort: (M−J)×G · Long: (J−M)×GShort gains when market falls (M>J). Long gains when market rises.
ONet P&L ($)Formula= L + NLocked margin. Should be small and stable once hedged. Large = hedge gap or basis move.
PEffectiveness ratioFormula= ABS(N/L)Only when both physical P&L and futures MTM are numeric. IFRS 9 requires 80–125%.
QEffectiveness statusFormula80–125% = Effective · 125–135% = Partial · else Ineffective80–125% = P&L in OCI. Outside range = ineffective portion in income statement immediately.
